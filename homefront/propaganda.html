Propaganda

Throughout WWII, the US government used propaganda to increase civilian commitment and support for the Allies. Propagandists used a wide variety of media including posters, advertisements, cartoons, comic books, leaflets, radio, books, films, animation, magazines, newspapers, and more. Common purposes of the propaganda were urging greater public effort for war production, persuading people to sell their materials for use in the war, and selling off war bonds.
