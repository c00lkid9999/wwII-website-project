Causes of WWII Research

  • Imposed heavy penalties on the defeated powers after the end of WWI. Most notable is the “War Guilt Clause”, in which the German nation accepted complete responsibility for starting the war. This meant that Germany was held liable for all material damages. The French in particular insisted on imposing enormous reparations. The treaty seemed unfair to the defeated powers, and they quickly violated the terms of the treaty. Ultimately this made far right parties such as the Nazi party more popular with the German public.
  • The League of Nations emerged from the Treaty of Versailles with 32 members, including most of the victors of WW1. It eventually expanded to contain Germany and other defeated countries. It was premised on the idea that a threat to one member demanded action from all members. This ultimately failed due to the fact that it required unanimous agreement before taking action, and the offending nation could be the one that vetoed. The League’s “great powers” were unable to respond to the threats and were also uninterested in addressing them, due to trauma and weakness from World War One. The League’s failure to respond only encouraged more invasions and aggression. The U.S. Senate rejected membership, which undermined the authority of the League. The withdrawal of other countries such as Germany, Italy, and Japan had the same effect.
  • The rise of fascism in Italy and Germany significantly contributed to the advent of World War Two. Facsism’s main ideas of nationalism, expansionism, and militarism caused fascist powers to invade other countries. It led to the belief that the people of the country were better than the rest of the world’s people. It also allowed the belief that it was morally correct and even necessary for the country to expand. For these reasons, fascist countries such as Italy, Germany, and Hungary began to annex parts of other countries in the late 1930s. This increased global tensions and plunged Europe into war.
  • Ultimately the main cause of World War Two was the expansionist ideologies of the Axis powers. These expansionist regimes made use of militaristic propaganda to convince their people that the country had to go to war to gain new territories. This is known as “Lebensraum” (living space) in Nazi Germany. This led to Italy’s conquest of Ethiopia, Japan’s invasion of Chinese Manchuria, and Germany’s annexation of Austria, Czechoslovakia, and Poland. These actions kickstarted the war in Europe and Asia.
  • Appeasement served only to exacerbate the likelihood of war through encouraging Hitler to continue with his expansionist goals. By tolerating territorial aggression from Germany, the United Kingdom and France unwittingly allowed the war to happen. British and French leaders hoped that Germany’s ambitions would settle down peacefully if they agreed to its demands. This backfired, as Germany learned that it could invade more countries with no pushback. Hitler himself reportedly said, “The forty-eight hours after the march into the Rhineland were the most nerve-wracking in my life. If the French had then marched into the Rhineland, we would have had to withdraw with our tails between our legs.”
